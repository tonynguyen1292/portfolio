About

I sit where the business meets the build.

My route here runs through three countries: a computer science degree in Ho Chi Minh City, a cybersecurity diploma in Canada, and a Master of Computer Science at Edith Cowan University here in Perth. Along the way I noticed that projects rarely fail in the code — they fail in the gap between what the business meant and what the team heard.

So I made that gap my specialty — from both sides. At Spirited Thinking I turned a client's idea for an AI image-classification system into functional specs and test cases the delivery team built against. At FPT I automated SAP reporting in ABAP after sitting with the people who actually ran the reports. In 2026 I put a credential behind each half: the ECBA from the IIBA on top of my Master of Computer Science — then shipped a portfolio the way real teams ship, with requirements catalogues, sprint backlogs, test suites and CI.

That's the profile: an engineer who writes the requirements, and an analyst who ships the build. Developers get specs they don't have to translate; the business gets software it can trace back to what it actually asked for.
